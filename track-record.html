Background

About Bibiani Gold Mine

Bibiani Gold Mine is one of Ghana's premier large-scale gold operations — a 3 Mtpa Carbon-in-Leach (CIL) processing facility located in the Bibiani-Anhwiaso-Bekwai district of the Western Region.

At the time of Brian Dunn's appointment, the operation was on care & maintenance following the Noble Mineral Resources IPO on the ASX. The mandate was to restore the operation to full production throughput.

Brian was appointed In-Country Managing Director — Africa, taking full responsibility for every aspect of the restart, from contractor mobilisation to full plant commissioning and stable production delivery.

BRADKY's Role

What was delivered

Operational Restart

Re-established all mining and processing activities from care & maintenance status. Full site mobilisation of personnel, contractors and equipment.

Production Ramp-Up

Achieved design throughput of 3 Mtpa at average grade of 2.15 g/t Au with 91% CIL recovery. Ramp-up completed within 2 years of C&M status.

Workforce Management

Managed a combined workforce of 500 direct employees and 1,200 contractors at full operation. Established local community employment and training programs.

Corporate Governance

Operating as an ASX-listed entity from an African base — reporting requirements, board governance and investor communications maintained throughout.
